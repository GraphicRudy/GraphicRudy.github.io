01 · Executive Summary

The product works. The scaffolding doesn't.

Karl's core loop — ask in English, get SQL, get an answer — is solid. The gaps are in onboarding, role clarity, error recovery, and mechanics users need to trust the system.

Why stakeholders should care

v0.1 ships with zero documented error states, no role matrix, and an unexplained activation mechanism the guide itself flags as vague. For a product promising "no SQL expertise required," the hidden complexity breaks the promise.

Four of the thirteen gaps — role matrix, CSV import format, error documentation, glossary on first use — account for the majority of predicted support friction. All shippable in v0.2 without touching the NL2SQL engine.

The v0.2 Recommendation

Karl doesn't need a redesign. It needs scaffolding.

The AI core is the product's moat. v0.2 should invest in the trust layer — role gating, error coverage, format docs, surfaces that tell users how the system thinks.

Ship the Quick Wins

G02, G04, G05, G07, G08, G13 — largest ticket reduction per unit of effort.

02

Scope the Strategic Bets
